harmony

Virtual Video Installation

This piece is created using Unity for the environment, and Touchdesigner and Adobe Premiere for the video. Set within a lush and vibrant floral forest, two video screens play within a clearing showcasing the rough lifestyle of factory metalworking through black and white film. I aim to question the validity of whether or not escapism has a place in today's society, and whether or not it can serve as beneficial to modern culture. For the people experiencing the virtual installation, I wanted them to reflect on their own life in modernity as they take in the office ambience and the factory work showcased on the screens. However, the lush floral forest around them serves as a binding force to convey a peaceful state, a sanctuary where one can feel at ease for acceptance and to rationalize the issues that cut deeper—mind over matter. Very similar to a meditative experience, but the piece doesn’t facilitate that process. Rather, it serves as a catalyst for questioning and reflection.
