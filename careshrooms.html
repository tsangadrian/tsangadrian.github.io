SJSU Creative Game Jam 2020: The Careshroom

Created by Adrian Tsang and Diana Tran

What you see here is my first attempt at creating a video game from scratch using Unity. For one week, this game jam was conducted with the theme ‘it matters’. Developed with one other partner, we sought to diverge from the traditional route of creating a game based on the concept of ‘physical matter’, dealing with topics such as atoms, molecules, and physical states. Our intention was to depict a game raising awareness for mental health by driving the message of ‘you matter’, bringing light that no human should be left behind. Self-worth and depression were themes we wanted to tackle, so our depiction was a mushroom society being brought into light their self-worth as the player takes on the role of spreading this idea. This project was extremely enriching in terms of learning Unity development, the process of game development, and also the mechanics of collaboration. In this project, I led the code and Unity development for the entire game, and worked on some of the assets as well. My partner led most of the asset creation. Honestly, this entire experience was insanely eye-opening as it showed what can be accomplished in such a short time frame, while also showing the volume of much you can learn.
